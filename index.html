♡ Response 1 (Due Feb. 09): Plato's Cave by Susan Sontag

To capture pictures is to capture a memory. I use photos and images to tell myself a story on what happened on a specific day or a specific time.

Almost to help myself relive it. It provides a comforting sense of a time where I was happy or excited. In the images I’ll share, there will always be a story behind it,

in which I was the happiest. These pictures are emotionally significant in my life. Sontag does point out how taking pictures does strip away from the experience itself, like “not living in the moment,”

how images are captured and taken, but aren’t lived. Although I do agree with the statement, the images afterwards help me replay the experience over and over again. I get the feeling of reminiscing

and the same feeling of being there again.

With the 9 projects, we can see the continuous joy of having company. Using new tools with each project, we can still come to the same conclusion.

everyone perceives an image differently. However, with the images I intend to take now reading Sontag’s writing, I want to capture a quiet and peaceful setting to determine societal values.

Not only do I want my photos to showcase the memories and experiences I’ve had, I also want them to notice dynamics or the emotions portrayed in the

images. Sontag does state how photos do freeze the experience, but the words explaining it might contradict people’s point of view. Photography is a beautiful way of peeking

into someone else’s mind and how they perceive the world.

♡ Response 2 (Due Feb. 24):‘AI slop’ distorting our reality, the world is sleepwalking into disaster by Nesrine Malik

The author of “With ‘AI slop’ distorting our reality, the world is walking into a big disaster with their eyes closed,” Nesrine Malik talks about how AI is becoming more normalized.

With creating new pictures or political messages, it bleeds into our daily lives. She talks about how there’s two sides of media in this day in age: real media and a parallel

media. Real media talks about the real world events, whereas the parallel AI media creates fabrications and fake information for our consumption. The parallel AI media creates a blur for

society, making it unable to read whether something is fact or fiction. Malik states how AI creates a bias for certain demographics creating false narratives for people

to read and believe.

We can also see how AI is constantly evolving to grab our attention with visuals and make us desensitized to outside information and we lose our desire to learn more about emotional

events happening in our world. AI makes these events more simplified or satire to help us move past the event without actually knowing about it. This makes human made work

or headlines more devalued, whereas entertainment has become more and more superior.

With AI, our workload has also been cut down. However, with AI, is it really a good thing? Our literacy and visual skills have been dumbed down with the rise and evolution of AI.

Malik understands the danger of AI and how oversimplifying everything about humanity, it would just slow us down. Making us emotionally, literacy, visually unavailable for society

and everything around us.
